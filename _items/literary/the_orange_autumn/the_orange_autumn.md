#The Orange Autumn

The cold wind had chilled my nose so much so that I had to cup my hands in front
of my face and puff, hoping to bring it back to normalcy. Early morning
auto-rickshaw trips in winter are not the things which I would treasure as
memories from my high-school days. The crude diesel engine was crying out with
some discord, having to pull a bulk of five persons on such a cold November
morning.  That desolate journey on a Sunday day break was as stereotypic in my
life as it was for the chai-wallas by the public-proclaimed bus stop to concoct
water, sugar, milk, and tea in proportions they would neither divulge nor err
about; a measure fixed in their habit owing to years of reckoning and
craftsmanship.

It is rather intriguing how much oblivious one can be when one has a monotonous
existence. I used to resolve to bring along a muffler as an aide during such
chilled encounters only to realize that I keep forgetting to search for it in
the closet. I am usually very systematic, but I am averse towards accessories.

Maybe, if I would have been sent to a boarding school as my father had wanted,
I would have turned up as a more sincere person. Given the sense of punctuality
and diligence, a valuable dividend would have been a great deal of discreteness
from my ever so intrusive parents. But that would have been a deprivation from
computers, the internet and a lot of tinkering around the household; from shirt
button pulleys and wheels to  matchbox dumpsters, from lemon and potato
batteries to origami and definitely from _Joy of Making Indian Toys_ and
_Space Adventures in Your Home_ and telescopes to superhero comics, animation
films and video games.

I shall remember that day as if it was yesterday. There is something about
morning golden hours in a city chocking up with particulate matter, that the
evenings cannot commensurate. I relish haze and dew and the smell of winter
evenings profoundly. However, the freshness of the morning breeze grazing over
your cheeks, nose, and forehead feels bustling among the bustle devoid
cityscape.

My eyes were watery. I don’t use glares usually even though I have always owned
a decent pair. And I am thankful for this wishful abstinence, for I could exult
my senses with the jubilant brilliance of the morning sunshine that had filled
the entire expanse of the light blue sky. The sun was hidden behind sparse
clouds, far yonder the horizons across the farmlands, buildings and ponds I had
been passing by. Yet its splendor extended across the entire expanse of the dust
bereft blue canvas, alike a watercolor painting, only alive and morphing.

I yearn to relive this autumn that was orange, alas only in my memories.
